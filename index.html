FAQ Section

1: What is the difference between major and independent music distribution?

Major music distribution involves releasing music through large record labels that provide extensive resources and support. Independent music distribution means artists release their music without major labels, usually using digital platforms, giving them more control and higher revenue shares but with more responsibilities.

How do independent artists distribute their music?

2: Independent artists use digital distribution services like Collabhouse to get their music on platforms like Spotify, Apple Music, Beatport and Amazon Music. They manage the release schedule, promotion and marketing themselves.

Is independent music distribution better than major distribution?

3: It depends on the artist's goals and resources. Independent distribution offers more control and higher revenue shares while major distribution provides more resources and broader reach. Each artist must decide what works best for their career.

Can independent artists make money from their music?

4: Yes, independent artists can make money from their music. They often get a higher percentage of royalties from streaming and sales compared to major label artists. However, they need to invest time and money into marketing and promotion to succeed.

What are the risks of independent music distribution?

5: The risks include financial burden, limited resources and challenges in gaining visibility. Independent artists must handle all aspects of their music career, which can be time-consuming and financially risky without guaranteed returns.

Conclusion

6: Choosing between major and independent music distribution is a significant decision for any artist. Independent music distribution provides control, flexibility and a higher share of your earnings while major distribution offers extensive resources and a wider reach. Understanding these differences can help you make the best choice for your career.
